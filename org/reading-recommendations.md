## Philosophy & Depth Thinking

### Iain McGilchrist — *The Master and His Emissary* (or *The Matter with Things*)
**Gap it fills**: You already work left-brain/right-brain territory in `being.md` and `expressiveness.md`, but without deep grounding. McGilchrist is a psychiatrist who spent decades analyzing hemispheric differences.

**Why it fits**: His thesis—that the left hemisphere sees parts/machines/explicit categories, while the right hemisphere sees wholes/contexts/living systems—will give your "being vs doing" and "expressiveness vs explicitness" dualities a neurological and phenomenological framework. Rigorous enough for your engineering mind; synthetic enough for your philosopher mind.

**Connections**:
- [Expressiveness](expressiveness.md) — Explicitness as left-hemisphere dominance; expressiveness as right
- [Being](being.md) — The architectural view of being maps to right-hemisphere "betweenness"
- [Duality](duality.md) — Hemispheric tension as another way to understand holding paradox

---

### Pierre Hadot — *Philosophy as a Way of Life*
**Gap it fills**: Your Stoicism references (in `being-human.md`, `ACT.md`) are present but undeveloped. Hadot argues ancient philosophy was never academic theory—it was a practice, a way of daily living.

**Why it fits**: Connects your `being.md` practices to a historical tradition. Reframes philosophy as *training* rather than *knowledge*, which maps directly onto your engineering mindset.

**Connections**:
- [Being Human](being-human.md) — Stoic dichotomy of control as a practice, not a concept
- [ACT](act.md) — Accept/Commit as modern descendent of ancient spiritual exercises
- [Meditation and Mindfulness](meditation-and-mindfulness.md) — Philosophy as attention-training

---

### Simone Weil — *Gravity and Grace* or *Waiting for God*
**Gap it fills**: Attention, labor, suffering, and the sacred in ordinary life. You don't have anything from the mid-20th century French spiritual/philosophical tradition.

**Why it fits**: She was a philosopher who worked in factories to understand labor; she wrote about attention as "the rarest and purest form of generosity." Her essay "The Love of God and Affliction" would resonate with `being-human.md` and `ACT.md`. Precise writer—no fluff.

**Connections**:
- [Being Human](being-human.md) — Affliction and the challenge of being human
- [Engagement](engagement.md) — Attention as the ground of relatedness and mastery
- [On Being a Piano Key](underground-piano-keys.md) — Weil's refusal of false consolation parallels Dostoevsky's underground man

### Søren Kierkegaard — *The Sickness Unto Death*
**Gap it fills**: Your "being" and "interiority" pieces are strong but don't yet engage the structural side of selfhood—despair as the inability to be oneself, the dizziness of freedom.

**Why it fits**: Kierkegaard is the original depth psychologist. His "infinite" vs "finite" self maps onto your "roles vs being" distinction. The concept of despair as a *category* of existence rather than a mood fits your systems-thinking approach.

**Connections**:
- [Interiority](interiority.md) — The layers of self; despair at the identity layer
- [Being](being.md) — Authenticity when the self is fluid
- [Will to Power](will-to-power.md) — Self-overcoming as response to despair

## Leadership & Systems Thinking

### Dave Snowden — Cynefin Framework
**Gap it fills**: Your engagement frameworks assume the right approach makes problems solvable. But leadership also requires *diagnosing* what kind of problem you're facing.

**Why it fits**: Cynefin distinguishes clear, complicated, complex, and chaotic domains. In complicated domains, experts solve; in complex domains, solutions *emerge* from experimentation. "Best practice" fails in complexity—you need probes and safe-to-fail experiments. Crucial for an engineering VP bridging technical and organizational leadership.

**Connections**:
- [Engagement](engagement.md) — How autonomy works differently in complex vs complicated contexts
- [Consensus](consensus.md) — When consensus is appropriate vs when experimentation is needed
- [OKRs](OKRs.md) — Goal-setting in complex domains requires outcome goals, not output goals

---

### Agnes Callard — *Aspiration: The Agency of Becoming*
**Gap it fills**: How do we become someone who values what we don't yet value? How do we learn to want things we can't yet properly want?

**Why it fits**: "Proleptic reasons"—acting on reasons you don't yet grasp because you're becoming the person who will grasp them—maps onto your will-to-power and learning-psychology threads. Excellent for interview/mentoring frameworks.

**Connections**:
- [Learning Psychology](learning-psychology.md) — The motivation to learn something you don't yet value
- [Engagement](engagement.md) — Mastery as aspiration, not just skill acquisition
- [Will to Power](will-to-power.md) — Becoming as creative self-overcoming

## Creative & Literary

### Fyodor Dostoevsky — *The Idiot* or *Demons*
**Gap it fills**: You have *Brothers K*, *Crime & Punishment*, and *Notes from Underground*—but not his other major works exploring ideology and authenticity.

**Why it fits**: *The Idiot* gives you Prince Myshkin, the inverse of your underground man—someone who *cannot* stop being authentic, to his ruin. *Demons* explores ideology as possession. Both extend your Dostoevsky thread naturally.

**Connections**:
- [On Being a Piano Key](underground-piano-keys.md) — Myshkin as the anti-underground man
- [Being](being.md) — Authenticity when it costs everything
- [Will to Power](will-to-power.md) — Ideology as corrupted will to power

---

### Marilynne Robinson — *Gilead* (and *Home*, *Lila*)
**Gap it fills**: Fiction that does what your nonfiction tries to do—make theological/philosophical inquiry embodied, personal, and grounded in ordinary life.

**Why it fits**: First-person novel from an aging Congregationalist minister writing to his young son. Luminous prose, never fluffy. Themes: grace, ordinary existence, presence, fatherhood. Given your Dostoevsky love and interest in interiority, Robinson feels like a natural extension.

**Connections**:
- [Interiority](interiority.md) — The minister's inner life as model of quiet depth
- [Being](being.md) — Presence in ordinary moments
- [Letter to My Unborn Son](letter.md) — Fatherhood, legacy, and witness

---

### Adrienne Rich — *Diving into the Wreck* (poetry) or *Arts of the Possible* (essays)
**Gap it fills**: Your poetry section has Dickinson and June Jordan but is otherwise thin. Rich is a rigorous intellectual who was also a poet.

**Why it fits**: Work on attention, witness, and entering history connects to your philosophical and leadership concerns. Her essay "When We Dead Awaken" on women and the creative process could be particularly interesting given your creative writing and technical career.

**Connections**:
- [Engagement](engagement.md) — Work and witness as relatedness
- [Words](../words/) — Poetry as act of attention
- [Being Human](being-human.md) — The body as site of political and personal reality

## Practice & Spirituality

### Thomas Merton — *New Seeds of Contemplation*
**Gap it fills**: You have meditation/mindfulness and Alan Watts, but not much from the Christian contemplative tradition that directly connects to your leadership and work life.

**Why it fits**: Merton was a Trappist monk who wrote about contemplation as the ground of action. He engaged deeply with Zen and was socially engaged. Contemplation-as-practice (not belief) is a useful frame for your meditation work.

**Connections**:
- [Meditation and Mindfulness](meditation-and-mindfulness.md) — Contemplation as attention-training across traditions
- [Being Human](being-human.md) — Silence and solitude as practices of being
- [Engagement](engagement.md) — Contemplation as the ground of authentic action
