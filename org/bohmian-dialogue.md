**Bohmian Dialogue** is a practice developed by quantum physicist **David Bohm** for conversation that moves beyond debate toward shared understanding.

Key features:

- **No fixed goal or agenda**: Participants don't seek decisions or outcomes—they explore thinking itself.
- **Suspension of assumptions**: Rather than defending beliefs, participants *observe and hold* their own assumptions up for inspection, without immediately acting on them.
- **Collective meaning-making**: The group is treated as a single "thinking system." The aim is to reveal the **tacit, shared thought structures** that shape how people see the world.
- **Deep listening & non-judgment**: Listening is primary; responses are offered tentatively, not as arguments to be won.
- **Slowness and patience**: Dialogue unfolds at a contemplative pace, allowing unspoken patterns and incoherencies in thought to surface.

Bohm distinguished *dialogue* (meaning "through the word," flowing inquiry) from *discussion* (which literally means "to break apart"—analytic, adversarial exchange).

In essence, it's a meditative form of conversation designed to make the *process of thought itself* visible and transform how people think together.
